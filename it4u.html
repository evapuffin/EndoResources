Skype for Business

Looking for a quick status update, or want show someone how to do something quickly? Skype for Business is your best option. You can easily and quickly send Instant Messages and share your screen with your colleagues as well.

SharePoint

Use SharePoint to work within documents at the same time as your colleagues. Collaborate and share files more efficiently by eliminating the need for multiple files moving between each other.

Teleconference

Want to host a phone bridge that can span the globe with colleagues, customers and/or vendors? Add your teleconference details to your meeting to join everyone together on the same call.

Desk and Conference Phones

Use your desk phone or a conference phone to join up to 4 callers (internal or external) onto the same phone call – no call-in credentials required!

WebEx

Having a meeting with multiple participants inside or outside the company? Use WebEx to share content, speak, and even show video all within one meeting. This is a great way to stay connected with colleagues, customers, and/or vendors around the world.

Microsoft Outlook

Outlook is an email and task management application where users can compose, send, receive and manage one or more email accounts from the same location. Users can create and manage contacts, calendars, tasks, take notes, keep a personal journal and more.

Microsoft Word

Word is a word-processing program that is used to create nearly any type of professional-quality document from reports, presentations, budgets, proposals and plans. You can use the program to build a library of forms such as invoices, statements, receipts, memos and agendas. Features within Word include spelling and grammar checks, text, font and page formatting, integration of images and HTML and more.

Microsoft PowerPoint

PowerPoint allows you to tell your story as a slideshow presentation. Users can create, collaborate and present their ideas in a dynamic and visually compelling way. Templates provide a built-in professional looking pages that can be used in the creation of a PowerPoint deck. Presenters can also attach notes to specific slides to assist during a presentation.

Microsoft Excel

Excel allows users to organize, format, manipulate and calculate data in a spreadsheet comprised of a grid of cells arranged in rows and columns. The program has a variety of features that include mathematical operations, graphing tools, pivot tables and macro programming to support statistical, financial and/or engineering activities. Data can also be displayed as charts, histograms and line graphs that can be embedded or added as a separate object.
